Create a single, self-contained, mobile-first HTML file (all CSS and JavaScript inline, no external libraries) that works as an interactive travel itinerary planner.

## Trip details
- Trip: Hanoi + Sapa, Vietnam — November 4 to November 10, 2026
- Nov 4: Arrive in Hanoi around lunchtime (~12PM). Night sleeper train to Sapa (Lao Cai) departing ~9:30PM from Hanoi Station.
- Nov 5: Train arrives Lao Cai ~6AM, shuttle up to Sapa (~1hr). Full day in Sapa.
- Nov 6: Full day in Sapa.
- Nov 7: Day in Sapa until evening. Shuttle down to Lao Cai, night sleeper train back to Hanoi departing ~9:30PM.
- Nov 8: Train arrives Hanoi ~5:30AM. Full day in Hanoi.
- Nov 9: Full day in Hanoi.
- Nov 10: Final day in Hanoi (departure day, airport ~45 min away).

## Book layout
1. One "page" per day (7 pages, Nov 4–10) arranged horizontally with CSS scroll-snap; swipe left/right between pages with snap-to-page. Fixed page indicator at the bottom: dots + "Day X of 7 · City", updating on scroll.
2. Each page has a ticket-style header (date, day number, city badge, route like "HANOI → LAO CAI") and a short day note (e.g., train departure reminders, November weather in Sapa: cool 10–18°C, misty, bring layers).
3. Each day is divided into 4 fixed time blocks: 5AM–8AM, 8AM–12PM, 12PM–5PM, 5PM–10PM. Always show all 4, even on travel days. Transit segments (flight, sleeper trains, shuttle, airport transfer) appear as visually distinct dashed "transit" strips inside the relevant block, not as cards.
4. Under each time block, 2–5 recommended places/activities matched to the time of day (sunrise spots/markets early, sightseeing mid-day, food/nightlife in the evening). Hanoi: Old Quarter, Hoan Kiem Lake, Train Street, Temple of Literature, egg coffee, water puppet theatre, pho/bun cha spots, Quang Ba flower market, West Lake, museums. Sapa: Fansipan cable car, Cat Cat Village, Muong Hoa Valley trek, Lao Chai/Ta Van, Silver & Love waterfalls, O Quy Ho Pass, night market, salmon hotpot, Red Dao herbal bath.

## Card design & behavior (important)
5. Each suggestion is a clean white rounded card with soft shadow: place name + one-line description on the left, and a CHECKBOX on the right.
6. SELECTION = checkbox only. Ticking the checkbox selects the card (whole card fills with the city accent color, checkbox inverts); unticking deselects. Clicking the card body must NOT change selection. Selection persists while swiping between pages (in-memory; do not use localStorage).
7. EXPAND = tapping the card body (or a small chevron tab). Each card has a slim full-width tab at its bottom center containing only a small ▼ chevron (no text) that flips when open. Tapping the card body or tab expands the card to reveal a photo section; tapping again collapses it.
8. PHOTO SECTION (inside the expanded area):
   - A horizontal, left-right scrollable strip of 5 photos (~150×110px thumbnails, scroll-snap, hidden scrollbar), hotlinked (never downloaded).
   - Photos load lazily the first time a card is expanded, with pulsing placeholder boxes and a "Loading photos…" status; results are cached per card. On failure show a friendly message and allow retry on next expand.
   - PRIMARY source (no API key): Wikimedia Commons search API (action=query, generator=search, gsrnamespace=6, origin=*, prop=imageinfo with iiurlwidth=480), querying "<name> <city>" then falling back to "<name> Vietnam"; filter to jpeg/png/webp/gif, keep relevance order, take 5.
   - OPTIONAL Google source: two constants at the top of the script, GOOGLE_API_KEY and GOOGLE_CX (default empty), with comments explaining how to get them (Google Custom Search JSON API + Programmable Search Engine, free tier 100 searches/day, key is exposed in the file so don't share publicly). If both are set, fetch Google's top 5 image results (searchType=image), using each result's full-size link with the Google-hosted thumbnailLink as onerror fallback. If the Google call fails or quota is exhausted, silently fall back to Wikimedia.
   - Below the photo strip, an always-present link "See more on Google Images →" opening https://www.google.com/search?tbm=isch&q=<URL-encoded name + city + Vietnam> in a new tab. This link lives ONLY inside the expandable section and must not trigger expand/collapse when clicked.

## Export feature
9. An "Export" button pinned to the top-right, always visible (fixed, high z-index), with a count badge showing how many cards are selected (hidden at zero).
10. Clicking Export downloads a CSV (Blob + temporary <a download>, UTF-8 BOM) containing ONLY selected cards, in itinerary order, with header: Date,Day,City,Time Block,Place/Activity,Description. Properly quote/escape fields. If nothing is selected, show a toast: "No places selected yet — tap a checkbox to add it to your plan." Show a success toast after export.

## Design requirements
- Mobile-first (~380px), touch targets ≥44px, readable sizes.
- Vietnam-inspired identity with per-city theming: Hanoi pages use lacquer red accents, Sapa pages use misty green accents, on a warm paper background, serif display type for dates. Selected vs unselected cards instantly distinguishable.
- Accessibility: keyboard-focus styles, aria-expanded on cards, aria-labels on checkboxes and Export, reduced-motion respected.
- No localStorage/sessionStorage. Everything in ONE .html file that opens directly in a mobile browser (photos and Google links need internet; the rest works offline).
